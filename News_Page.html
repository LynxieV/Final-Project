Alzheimer's Destroys The Brain Cells That Keep Us Awake

Summarized from Science Alert . Source date: 8/14/19.

Date: August 14, 2019.

Written by Amani Nelson.

This article goes into detail about a recent study from the University of California San Francisco (UCSF). UCSF has found evidence to supportthat Altzheimer's is destroying the brain cells that keep us awake. The evidence comes from an analysis of post-mortum brain cells of thirteen patients with Altzheimers and 7 people who have healthy braincells. According to lead author Jun Oh, who researches memory and ageing at UCSF, "[The degeneration is] remarkable because it's not just a single brain nucleus that's degenerating, but the whole wakefulness-promoting network." In other words, this study has revealed another way that Altzheimer's can be identified.

Fast Fact: The name originates from a 1960's German physician, Dr. Alois Alzheimer, who treated a woman who experienced the symptoms that today we call Altzheimer's disease.

Potential Dementia Repellant: Controlling Blood Pressure

Summarized from CBS News . Source date: 8/13/19.

Date: August 14, 2019.

Written by Amani Nelson.

This article discusses how a study conducted by the U.S. National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke (NINDS) has noticed that taking action to reduce high blood pressure with prevention or intensive treatment for it, can reduce the risk of potential neurological disfunction. In fact, during the release of these findings Dr. Richard Hodes, who is the director of the U.S. National Institute on Aging said, "These findings [are] encouraging as we continue to advance the science of understanding and addressing the complexities of brain diseases such as Alzheimer's and related dementias." Overall this article, is interesting because it captures another milestone in the quest to understanding dementia fully.


Fast Fact: Dementia is an umbrella term for Altzheimer's because the disease itself captures most of the symptoms of dementia.
